Design principles

In case you want to learn a little more, below are a handful of guiding principles that I use in my work:

  1. The fundamentals of human-centered design can and should be used by everyone. This is how we build meaningfully for people.
  2. Companies should exist to make the world better in some way. And I want to be a part of the revolution, no matter how small or quiet.
  3. Empathy, research, and testing are vital to good design. They help identify unmet human needs and solve them meaningfully.
  4. Design should be intentional and essential. Every decision should be carefully considered and deliberate, the right amount of design for the problem.
  5. Interactions should bring delight — whether overt or a subconscious relief at the ease of an action.
  6. Collaboration and candid feedback make the work stronger. My job is to facilitate these conversations and use them to find the best way forward.
